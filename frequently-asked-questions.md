## Frequently asked questions

1. **What is the maximum length of a Reel?**
The maximum length of a Reel is 20 minutes. Instagram limits recommendation to non-followers to Reels of 3 minutes or less.

1. **Can you post a Reel from a computer?**
Yes. Sign in to Instagram on the web, click **Create**, click **Post**, then upload one video file. Instagram publishes a single-file video upload as a Reel. An upload of two or more files publishes as a carousel post instead of a Reel.

1. **Can you edit a Reel after you post it?**
You can edit the caption, the tagged accounts, and the location after you post a Reel. You cannot edit the video content, the trim points, or the audio track after you post a Reel.

1. **What is the difference between Share to Feed on and Share to Feed off?**
Setting **Share to Feed** to on publishes the Reel to the home feed of your followers, in addition to the Reels tab and the Explore page. Setting **Share to Feed** to off limits the Reel to the Reels tab and the Explore page.

1. **Can other accounts reuse your original audio?**
Yes, by default, on a public account. To change this permission, open **Settings**, select **Original Audio**, then set the **Reels** toggle to off.

1. **Do Reels expire like Stories?**
No. A Reel remains on your profile until you delete it. A Story expires 24 hours after you post it, unless you save the Story to your Highlights.

1. **Can you create a Reel from a comment?**
Yes. On a Reel, select **Reply** on a comment, then select the camera icon. The comment appears as a sticker that you can add to a Reel you record or upload.
